# Alternatives Research Guide

This guide helps you fill out `alternatives_template.json` with product alternatives for each category tier.

## Understanding the Tiers

### Cheaper Tier
Budget-friendly options that save money with acceptable trade-offs.

**What to look for:**
- Store brands / generic options
- Conventional (non-organic) products
- Bulk sizes for better per-unit pricing
- Frozen alternatives (often cheaper than fresh)

**Trade-offs to document:**
- May not be organic
- Potentially more packaging
- Less local sourcing
- Fewer ethical certifications

### Balanced Tier
Good value products with reasonable sustainability practices.

**What to look for:**
- Mid-tier brands with some sustainability commitments
- Products with 1-2 certifications
- Reasonable packaging (recyclable at minimum)
- Regional sourcing when available

**Notes to include:**
- Why this is a good middle-ground choice
- Any partial certifications or commitments
- Seasonal availability considerations

### Conscious Tier
Premium sustainable and ethical choices for maximum positive impact.

**What to look for:**
- Full organic certification
- Fair trade certification (where applicable)
- Minimal/compostable packaging
- Local or regenerative sourcing
- B-Corp or similar company certifications
- Worker welfare commitments

## Certifications to Look For

### Food & Agriculture
| Certification | What It Means |
|--------------|---------------|
| USDA Organic | No synthetic pesticides, non-GMO |
| Fair Trade Certified | Fair wages, safe conditions |
| Regenerative Organic Certified | Soil health, animal welfare, social fairness |
| Rainforest Alliance | Sustainable farming, biodiversity |
| Non-GMO Project Verified | No genetically modified ingredients |
| Certified Humane | Animal welfare standards |
| Animal Welfare Approved | Highest animal welfare standards |

### Packaging & Environment
| Certification | What It Means |
|--------------|---------------|
| How2Recycle | Clear recycling instructions |
| Compostable (BPI) | Industrially compostable packaging |
| FSC Certified | Sustainable forestry for paper/cardboard |
| Ocean Bound Plastic | Made from recovered ocean-bound plastic |

### Company-Level
| Certification | What It Means |
|--------------|---------------|
| B-Corp | Meets social & environmental standards |
| 1% for the Planet | Donates 1% revenue to environmental causes |
| Climate Neutral | Carbon neutral operations |

## Where to Research

### Price Comparison
- **FreshDirect** - Your current retailer (baseline prices)
- **Whole Foods** - Premium organic options
- **Trader Joe's** - Budget-friendly alternatives
- **Costco** - Bulk organic options
- **Thrive Market** - Online natural/organic retailer

### Brand Information
- **Brand websites** - Look for "About Us", "Our Story", "Sustainability"
- **B-Corp Directory** - bcorporation.net/directory
- **EWG Food Scores** - ewg.org/foodscores
- **Good On You** - goodonyou.eco (primarily fashion but expanding)

### Local Options
- **LocalHarvest** - localharvest.org (find local farms)
- **Farmers Market Coalition** - farmersmarketcoalition.org
- **Regional CSA directories**

## Research Checklist

For each category, gather:

- [ ] **Brand name** - Full official name
- [ ] **Estimated price** - Current retail price (check FreshDirect, Whole Foods)
- [ ] **Packaging type** - Plastic, glass, cardboard, compostable, etc.
- [ ] **Certifications** - List all relevant certifications
- [ ] **Source URL** - Link to product page or brand website
- [ ] **Why this tier** - Brief explanation of tier placement

## Category-Specific Notes

### Produce Categories
For produce (greens, onions, root_veg, tropical, tomatoes, peppers, squash, beans):
- **Cheaper**: Conventional from major distributors
- **Balanced**: Organic from regional farms
- **Conscious**: Local, regenerative, or biodynamic farms

Consider documenting:
- Seasonality (when is this available locally?)
- Storage tips for bulk buying
- Frozen alternatives for off-season

### Milk
- **Cheaper**: Conventional store brand
- **Balanced**: Organic from regional dairy
- **Conscious**: Pasture-raised, A2, or regenerative dairy

Consider:
- Bottle return programs (glass vs plastic)
- Local creamery options
- Plant-based alternatives if relevant

### Fruit Berries
- **Cheaper**: Conventional, frozen options
- **Balanced**: Organic from major brands (Driscoll's Organic)
- **Conscious**: Local farms, farm stands, pick-your-own

Consider:
- Frozen berries are often more affordable AND sustainable (less spoilage)
- Seasonal availability varies greatly

## Tips for Efficient Research

1. **Start with your current purchases** - Check what brands you already buy
2. **Use retailer websites** - Filter by organic/certification
3. **Check multiple retailers** - Prices vary significantly
4. **Note seasonal items** - Some alternatives only available certain times
5. **Document sources** - Keep URLs for future reference

Good luck with your research!
